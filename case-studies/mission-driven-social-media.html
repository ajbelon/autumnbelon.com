Waste to Taste

Goal: Increase awareness of food rescue initiatives and encourage community participation.

Strategy: Introduced video content, consistent posting cadence, and brand-aligned visuals to highlight impact stories and volunteer opportunities.

Tactics & Approach:

  • Published 3–4 posts per week highlighting rescued meals and volunteer impact.
  • Created short-form videos and reels showcasing community events.
  • Used branded graphics for program updates to maintain a recognizable visual identity.
  • Leveraged audience feedback via polls and Q&A sessions to shape content and increase engagement.
  • Cross-posted content on Facebook and Instagram for maximum reach and consistency.

Engagement & Lessons Learned:

  • Content emphasizing human stories and visual impact drove 94.5% increase in Facebook Views.
  • Iterative content adjustments based on audience interaction metrics improved organic reach without paid ads.

Little Free Pantry

Goal: Grow visibility, engagement, and donations for the pantry program.

Strategy: Used social media to educate the public about the program, promote events, and communicate community needs.

Tactics & Approach:

  • Published 3–4 posts per week sharing program updates, event announcements, and community needs.
  • Created clear, visually consistent graphics explaining how the pantry works and how to participate or donate.
  • Shared schedules and highlights of pantry events to keep audiences informed.
  • Used polls, Q&As, and comment prompts to understand audience interests and increase engagement.
  • Cross-posted content on Facebook and Instagram to maintain visibility and consistency across platforms.

Engagement & Lessons Learned:

  • Educational content explaining program operations drove 331.8% increase in Facebook Views.
  • Clear communication of needs and events contributed to +4.1K% engagement growth.
  • Consistent, transparent updates helped humanize the organization and strengthened community trust.

Burrito Brigade

Goal: Improve engagement, storytelling, and community awareness for the flagship program.

Strategy: Leveraged social media to educate the public about program operations, highlight events, and communicate community impact.

Tactics & Approach:

  • Regular posts 3–5 times per week sharing updates, event announcements, and program highlights.
  • Created visually consistent posts explaining program processes and how the community can contribute.
  • Shared information about Burrito Brigade events and initiatives to increase awareness and participation.
  • Used interactive posts (polls, Q&As) to engage audiences and gather feedback for content refinement.
  • Maintained cross-platform consistency to maximize reach and reinforce organizational branding.

Engagement & Lessons Learned:

  • Clear educational content led to a 129.5% increase in Facebook Views.
  • Transparent communication and regular updates improved audience engagement by 157.8%.
  • Consistent program education helped humanize the organization and strengthened community trust.

Key Takeaways & Strategy Insights

Across all Burrito Brigade initiatives, a strategic, educational approach to social media led to strong engagement, community awareness, and tangible program impact. Here’s what made the strategy effective:

  • Consistent Posting Cadence: Regularly scheduled content (3–5 posts per week) kept the community informed and engaged.
  • Program Education: Posts explained how each initiative works, highlighting processes, events, and needs to help audiences understand and connect with the organization’s mission.
  • Transparent Communication: Sharing updates, event announcements, and program milestones built trust and strengthened community perception.
  • Interactive & Informative Content: Polls, Q&As, and calls-to-action encouraged audience participation and helped guide content based on community interest.
  • Data-Driven Iteration: Tracking engagement metrics informed which types of educational content resonated most, allowing continual improvement.
  • Cross-Platform Consistency: Coordinated posts across Facebook and Instagram ensured maximum reach while reinforcing program messaging and branding.
  • Humanizing Through Education: By clearly communicating program operations, events, and needs, social media served as a tool to make the organization relatable and understandable to the broader public.

By combining strategic scheduling, clear program education, and community-focused communication, Burrito Brigade programs increased awareness, engagement, and participation — all organically.
